- The Committee on Civil Liberties visited 26 detention centers in 10 EU Member States to assess the implementation of Directive 2003/9/EC on minimum standards for the reception of asylum seekers.
- The committee observed a lack of hygiene, decay, and failure to respect human dignity in certain centers.
- Access to healthcare and children's right to education were not always respected.
- The committee calls for clear, fair, effective, and proportionate asylum procedures and a permanent system of visits and inspections of detention centers.
- The committee emphasizes the collective responsibility of EU Member States in addressing issues in detention centers and calls for European solidarity in the area of asylum.
