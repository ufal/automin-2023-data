- The sitting was opened at 9 a.m.
- Daniel Cohn-Bendit questioned the decision to control photography during President Sarkozy's speech and requested clarification on who is in charge of photography at the European Parliament.
- The President assured Cohn-Bendit that he would receive an answer before the start of the sitting.
- Astrid Lulling, as Quaestor of the Parliament, raised concerns about the route to the Parliament entrance being blocked by riot police, causing difficulties for Members to enter.
- The President acknowledged the issue and assured that it would be taken into account for Members who may arrive late and miss voting.
