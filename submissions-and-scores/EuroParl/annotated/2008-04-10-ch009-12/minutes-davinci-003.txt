• The European Union adopted the Code of Conduct on Arms Exports in 1998.
• The Council and Commission have expressed satisfaction with the traditionally good relations and cooperation between the Council's Conventional Arms Exports Working Group and the European Parliament's Subcommittee on Security and Defence.
• The European Union has made annual reports on completed arms transfers since 1998.
• The European Union has defined its own list of arms and military equipment to be covered by the code and started more serious coordination of the policy for export to individual third countries.
• The European Union has adopted guidelines for the brokering of arms trading in 2001, and the common position was adopted in 2003.
• The European Council has recently adopted a decision to support the international agreement on arms trade.
• The Commission is taking concrete steps to combat illegal trafficking in arms by air, targeting particularly aircraft travelling to and within Africa.
• The Commission is taking steps to enhance rules on record keeping and marking, to develop an export/import system for firearms for civilian use, and to improve cooperation between national law enforcement administrations.
• The European Council has dragged its feet on transforming the EU code of conduct on arms transfer into a legally binding document.
