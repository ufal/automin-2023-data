- Charles Tannock proposes amendments to the meeting on the situation of Burmese refugees in Thailand.
- Paragraph 2 amendment: addition of "as well as deliberate impoverishment, arbitrary taxation and land confiscation."
- Paragraph 5 amendment: addition of "calls for immediate and full access to all the detained Rohingya boat people in order to define their protection needs."
- Paragraph 6 amendment: emphasizes the boat people issue as a regional one and calls for increased cooperation among regional neighbors.
- Recital E amendment: mentions the United Nations refugee agency's concern about the mistreatment of Burmese refugees and their access to some of the 126 Rohingya still in Thai custody.
