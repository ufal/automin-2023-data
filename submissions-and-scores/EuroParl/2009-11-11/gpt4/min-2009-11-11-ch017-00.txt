- The Services Directive is a wide-ranging initiative that requires various measures from Member States, including legislation and cooperation facilitation.
- Setting up points of single contact and ensuring their functionality is a significant challenge.
- Stakeholder involvement is crucial for understanding and communicating the directive's advantages and addressing concerns.
- The implementation deadline is approaching, with just over half of Member States expected to achieve it by the end of 2009 or early 2010.
- Points of single contact should eventually become fully-fledged e-government centers, providing additional information and procedures.
