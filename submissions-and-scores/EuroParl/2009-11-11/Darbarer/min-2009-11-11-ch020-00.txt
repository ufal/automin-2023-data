Date: 25.4.2022

🔷 Abolition of visa regime
	🔹 Tanja Fajon, a member of the Committee on Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs, presented a report on the proposal for a Council regulation amending Council Regulation No 539/2001 on third country nationals subject to or exempt from a visa requirement when crossing external borders as well as those exempt from that requirement
	🔹 The European Parliament supports the abolition of the visa regime for all the Western Balkans and calls on the European Commission to prepare as soon as possible a proposal for visa abolition for Macedonia, Serbia and Montenegro.

🔷 Implementation of criteria
	🔹 Jacques Barrot confirms the Commission's commitment to monitor closely the fulfilment of the criteria laid down in the road map
	🔹 Jacques Barrot invites Parliament to be a colegislator in the field of visas.

🔷 Discussion of visa liberalisation
	💬 Anna Maria Corazza Bildt, Claude Moraes, Ulrike Lunacek, Doris Pack and Sarah Ludford are discussing visa liberalisation for Western Balkans.

🔷 Abolition of short-stay visa requirement
	📅 The European Parliament will vote on the abolition of the short-stay visa requirement for Balkan countries tomorrow
	🔹 Kinga Gál, Zoran Thaler, Axel Voss and Emine Bozkurt are in favour of the proposal.

🔷 Improvement of visa-free travel
	🔹 Macedonia, Serbia and Montenegro abolished their visas on 19 December
	🔹 Elżbieta Katarzyna Łukacijewska, Cristian Dan Preda, Jacques Barrot and Tanja Fajon are in favour of visa-free travel for Western Balkans.

🔷 Control of borders
	🔹 It is 20 years since the fall of the Berlin Wall
	🔹 Hungarians living in Vojvodina want to be able to cross borders without any barrier or visa.
