- The Prüm Convention aims to deepen cooperation between EU countries to counter terrorism, cross-border crime, and illegal immigration.
- The initiative involves the collection, storage, and sharing of DNA data and fingerprints.
- Data protection standards set out in Chapter VI of the Prüm initiative are not self-sufficient and need a complete and general framework.
- A clear and effective judicial framework for data protection is essential.
- Cooperation between border control authorities, customs, police, and security authorities needs to be improved to ensure the safety of Europeans.
