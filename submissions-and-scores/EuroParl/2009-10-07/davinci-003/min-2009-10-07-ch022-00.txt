The Commission must take action to ensure that passengers are adequately protected in the event of airline bankruptcy.

- Debate on passenger compensation in the event of airline bankruptcy
- 77 airline bankruptcies in the EU since 2000
- Passengers stranded at destinations without accommodation or flights home
- Many passengers unable to receive compensation due to booking online
- Denied boarding compensation, rights to assistance, and tougher laws on ticket pricing transparency already in place
- Loophole exists for passengers who book online
- Idea of establishing a reserve compensation fund floated
- Commissioner Tajani reiterates priority of protecting passengers in all transport sectors
- Major study conducted on consequences of airline bankruptcy
- Commission to analyze various possible options for reimbursement and repatriation
- Public consultation process to be set in motion
- Commission to analyze impact of various possible solutions on consumers and air transport sector
