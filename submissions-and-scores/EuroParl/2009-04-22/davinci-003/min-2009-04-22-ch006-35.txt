- Summarize the meeting:

- The meeting discussed the Common rules for the internal market in electricity.
- The rapporteur, Eluned Morgan, explained why Parliament had moved from its first-reading position on the Electricity and Gas Directive.
- The report as it stands is far from perfect, but substantial gains for energy consumers in the European Union have been made.
- Energy poverty has been put onto the European agenda for the first time.
- The independence of the national regulatory authorities has been ensured and they have been given an increased role to police the new system.
- The Commission and competition authorities have been encouraged to continue their work in ensuring that companies that own both generation and transmission systems play by the rules.
- The rapporteur thanked colleagues for all their help over the years.
