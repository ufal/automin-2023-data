- Parliament has moved from its first-reading position on the Electricity and Gas Directive.
- Substantial gains have been made for energy consumers in the European Union.
- The issue of energy poverty has been put onto the European agenda for the first time.
- Independence of national regulatory authorities has been ensured, with an increased role to police the new system.
- The Commission and competition authorities are encouraged to ensure that companies owning both generation and transmission systems play by the rules and do not abuse their positions.
