- Europol is seen as unnecessary from an objective point of view, but the EU believes it is essential to have its own police force.
- Europol officers have immunity from prosecution for anything they do or say in the course of their duties, which is a concept alien to English law.
- The EU is creating its own judicial system with instruments such as the European arrest warrant and trials in absentia, which is causing the destruction of basic liberties in Britain.
- The issue of data protection has not been resolved with regard to plans to grant all authorities unlimited access to data.
- Europol is important in combating criminality, especially in an area of liberty and free movement within the European Union.
