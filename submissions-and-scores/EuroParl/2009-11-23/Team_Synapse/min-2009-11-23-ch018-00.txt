SUMMARY
- The next item is the joint debate on the following reports: on the draft Council Decision adopting the rules on the confidentiality of Europol information and on the list of third States and organisations with which Europol shall conclude agreements.
- The initiative of the Kingdom of Belgium, the Czech Republic, Germany, Spain, the French Republic, Hungary, the Netherlands, the Slovak Republic, the Republic of Finland, Sweden and the UK is also discussed.
- Timothy Kirkhope is the rapporteur of the Committee on Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs.
- His report on the draft Council Decision adopting the rules on the confidentiality of Europol information calls for a rejection of the text.
- The report on accreditation of forensic laboratory activities on the initiative of Sweden and Spain calls for the proposal to be withdrawn.
- The Lisbon Treaty will be in force 7 days and 5 hours from now.
- The Council is in a rush to adopt a series of decisions.
- The rapporteur for Europol and the transfer of personal data and confidential documents to third parties would like to hear from the Council on the issue.
- The European Parliament rejected the Council's proposals concerning Europol's work on the basis of the Treaty of Lisbon.
- The implementation of European internal and security policy has been a task reserved exclusively for the executive.
- There is no international consensus on minimum standards in relation to fundamental rights and legal protection.
- Common minimum standards must be put in place on the protection of personal data between the EU and third countries.
- The European Parliament wants Parliament and the Council to be on an equal footing and in balance with each other in the decision-making process regarding the Europol package.
- Mr President, Mr Vice-President of the Commission, Mr President and Mr Member reject the proposal.
- The European Crime Prevention Network was set up in 2001 by Council Decision 2001/427/JHA.
- Its objectives were to facilitate cooperation and exchanges of information and experiences at national and European level, collect and analyse relevant information aimed at the exchange of best practices, organise conferences, seminars, meetings and initiatives and provide expertise to the Council and the Commission concerning crime prevention.
- In 2005, the network underwent its first internal structural reform.
- In March 2009, an external evaluation was published concerning the functioning of the network.
- The problems brought to light included the lack of suitable resources, an ineffective secretariat, lack of commitment on the part of the national representatives and a poor work programme.
- The evaluation also considered Parliament has rejected the draft Council Decisions on Europol and forensic laboratories.
- The Commission is prepared to provide financial support for the accreditation of scientific police laboratories in accordance with provisions for financial regulations.
- It is in favour of using accreditation because it encourages a higher quality of work from these laboratories.
- The Commission is willing to evaluate the implementation and application of the instrument for 1 July 2018.
- The European Union Crime Prevention Network has encountered strategic, political and organisational problems.
- The Commission has increased financial support to the network's secretariat.
- Parliament will play its full role in the new strategy against organised crime and all forms of crime.
- The whole House wants the Commission to revise the legislative package on Europol.
- The next Commissioner will be asked if she is committed to drawing up legislative proposals on this matter in order for Parliament to legislate.
- The Group of the Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe wants the pooling of resources to fight organised crime and trafficking to be strictly controlled.
- The European Parliament has been asked to examine four texts on Europol before the entry into force of the Treaty of Lisbon.
- The texts concern the issue of protecting the confidentiality of data passed on by Europol, including to third countries.
- The GUE/NGL Group calls on the Council and the Commission to withdraw these proposals.
- PERSON113 congratulates the Vice-President of the Commission for his work as the Commissioner responsible for this area.
- He visited the office in his country responsible for Europol, relations with Europol as well as the Schengen area.
- MEPs will vote against the Council's proposal for exchanges of information between Europol and partners, including third countries.
- Parliament rejects the proposals of the Council and the Commission.
- Parliament wants to take part in the decision-making process.
- Europol plays a pivotal role in terms of the exchange of information and provides a focus for existing police cooperation networks.
- People-trafficking is Europe's greatest failure.
- PERSON120 and PERSON121 discuss the Treaty of Lisbon, the SWIFT codes agreement and the European Crime Prevention Network.
- PERSON122 is the shadow rapporteur of one of the dossiers on Europol.
- He thinks Parliament should have greater powers over Europol's activities in order to check that they do not undermine citizens' rights.
- PERSON123 wants to create a common legal framework setting out standards for the activities of forensic laboratories across the European Union.
- PERSON124 wants to stress the importance of the Council framework decision obliging all forensic laboratories supplying the results of genetic and fingerprint tests to observe the ISO 17025 international standard.
- PERSON125 wants to draw attention to Article 15 of the decision adopting the implementing rules governing Europol's relations with partners.
- There will be other texts on Europol and Parliament will be colegislator.
- It would be difficult to stop Europol from becoming an agency.
- The Commission believes we need an in-depth, case-by-case analysis to determine whether to change the third pillar acquis.
- Parliament's role will have a positive impact on all future texts.
