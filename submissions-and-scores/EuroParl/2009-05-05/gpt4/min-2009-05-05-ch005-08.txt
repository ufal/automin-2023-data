- The meeting marked the fifth anniversary of the largest enlargement in the history of the European Union, which included ten countries from Central and Eastern Europe and the Mediterranean.
- The enlargement was considered a great success for the EU, contributing to democracy, stability, and improved standards of living in the new Member States.
- The accession of twelve new countries has strengthened the EU's position worldwide and increased its influence as an international political and economic actor.
- The Treaty of Lisbon contains core reforms necessary to adapt the EU's institutions to the consequences of enlargement and to meet future challenges.
- The meeting also expressed concern for the situation of US-Iranian journalist Roxana Saberi, who was sentenced to eight years in prison for allegedly spying on behalf of the US, and demanded her immediate and unconditional release.
