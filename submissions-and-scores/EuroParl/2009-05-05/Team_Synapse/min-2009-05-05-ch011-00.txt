- The next item is Question Time (B6-0231/2009).
- The Commission considers the maintenance of non-transparent systems of income taxation within the European Union to be compatible with free competition.
- Member States are free to design their direct tax systems in a way that best meets their domestic policy objectives and requirements.
- The European Commission adopted a communication on 28 April 2009 to promote good governance in the tax area.
- There is a Treaty and the Treaty is very clear.
- The Commission has no intention of writing a new treaty with different responsibilities.
- In November 2007 Parliament adopted resolution (P6_TA(2007)0507) on an integrated approach to earthquakes by the EU (prevention, response and repairing damage).
- The Commission recently adopted a communication entitled 'A Community approach on the prevention of natural and manmade disasters' which also covers earthquakes.
- There was a devastating earthquake in Italy.

- Several warning devices have been installed there.

- According to the Institute of Energy of South East Europe, many EU countries and other countries from southern and eastern regions and eastern Mediterranean countries are looking for nuclear power stations.
- PERSON317 congratulates Commissioner on the nuclear package she has just tabled.
- She would like to know if she could prepare a proposal for an EU regulatory authority or the upgrading of WENRA to a body for issuing binding standards in the next legislative period and if they could also make IAEA standards compulsory for all Member States.
- PERSON320 is proud of the Covenant of Mayors.
- In 2008, the Commission supported the initiative from cities to have a Covenant.
- The Commission negotiates with other national, regional and local administrations to ensure their support for the smaller cities.
- For the period 2007-2013, EUR 9 billion from cohesion policy funds will be directly invested in sustainable energy initiatives in the EU.
- By the end of this year, the Commission is planning to develop a new energy efficiency action plan and a communication on the financing of low-carbon energy sources.
- The EU has taken a firm stance against straight protectionism since the outbreak of the economic crisis.
- The Commission is intensively monitoring protectionist measures put in place by its major trading partners with a view to appropriate action.
- Developments in Russia, Indonesia and Argentina need close monitoring.
- PERSON328 asks about the Commission's monitoring of trade defence instruments and Internet piracy in Canada.
- The Commission is aware of the issues raised in relation to Canada's intellectual property rights protection and enforcement.
- It is taking the matter up with the Canadian authorities at the highest level and will call for revision of Canadian copyright law.
- After two years of negotiations, the EU has a free trade agreement with Korea within reach.
- The agreement would eliminate EUR 1.6 billion of customs duties to the benefit of EU exporters.
- It would also provide significant new market access in services of special interest to EU service providers.
- The most substantial barriers will be removed from day one of the agreement.
- The Commission is consulting on the best way forward.
- The Commission is engaged in negotiations with the United States to resolve the long-running dispute over hormone-treated beef.
- The imposition of the so-called 'Carousel' sanctions by the US on certain EU products will be postponed by a further two weeks beyond the original 23 April deadline.
- The Commission has planned another meeting for the 14th of this month.
