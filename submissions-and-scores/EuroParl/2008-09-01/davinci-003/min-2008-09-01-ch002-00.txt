- European satellite navigation programmes EGNOS and Galileo are being signed into law
- The regulation is a signal reminder that many aims can only be achieved in the framework of the European Union and not by individual Member States acting alone
- Galileo will comprise a network of 30 satellites and a ground-control infrastructure
- The European institutions agreed on Community funding of EUR 3.4 billion
- The Galileo Interinstitutional Panel (GIP) will meet to discuss the conditions necessary for the successful implementation of the programme
- The Galileo Regulation was signed by the President of the European Parliament, the representative of the Council, the Commissioner, the Chairman of the Committee on Industry, Research and Energy, and the rapporteur
