- Lithuanian airline FlyLAL filed for bankruptcy, joining tens of other European airlines that went bankrupt this year
- European Parliament legislation provides guarantees and compensation for passengers if an airline is at fault for delays or luggage issues, but not if the airline is experiencing financial difficulties
- The Commission recommends that governments return money to passengers for purchased tickets when an airline goes bankrupt, then recoup the money from the bankrupt company
- A reserve compensation fund has been proposed by Mr. Simpson
- The European Parliament and the Council are working on general rules for the supply of air transport services to contribute to passenger security
