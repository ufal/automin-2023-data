15. Order of business

The next item was the order of business.
The final draft agenda for the October 2009 sittings (PE428.982/PDOJ) had
been distributed and a number of changes had been proposed (Rule 140):

Monday

The following spoke: Bruno Gollnisch, who regretted  that the matter of
parliamentary immunity would not be debated (the President replied that he
had received no request to hold such a debate).

Tuesday

No changes.
Wednesday

Request from the PPE Group to change the title of the motions for
resolutions on freedom of information in Italy and other EU Member States
to 'Freedom of information in the European Union'.

The following spoke: Simon Busuttil, on behalf of the PPE Group, who moved
the request, Manfred Weber, on behalf of the PPE Group, to support the
request, and Hannes Swoboda, on behalf of the S&D Group, against the
request.

Parliament rejected the request by RCV (195 for, 231 against, 9
abstentions).

Thursday

No changes.
The order of business was thus established.
