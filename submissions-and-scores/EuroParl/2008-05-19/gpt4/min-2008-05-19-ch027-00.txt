- The meeting discussed the importance of creating a good environment for businesses and consumers in the European market.
- Collective redress was a focus, with half of the 10 benchmarks relating to avoiding punitive damages and expensive procedures.
- The need for open-mindedness and communication was emphasized, with upcoming stakeholders' conferences involving businesses, consumers, and academics.
- The growing area of cross-border financial services was mentioned as a sector that needs a cross-border collective redress system to make institutions more responsible and careful.
- The importance of consumer awareness and confidence in the European market was highlighted, with a call for a European Consumer Ombudsman and a focus on digital sectors.
