- Parliament voted for the first time within the framework of the new regulatory procedure with scrutiny on whether to reject a Commission legislative proposal.
- The vote was related to the Capital Requirements Directive.
- The European Parliament aimed to send a clear signal to the Commission, demanding an appropriate balance between matters proposed by the Commission in the codecision procedure and matters handled by the Commission alone within the comitology procedure.
- The Commission made concrete proposals within the comitology procedure relating to credit rating agencies, which were considered to go beyond technical aspects and needed to be returned to the codecision procedure.
- The Parliament aimed to discuss everything jointly in the codecision procedure with a clear focus.
