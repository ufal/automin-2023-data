1. Mrs Haug presented a comprehensive, demanding and brave budgetary report which was voted through unanimously in the Committee on Budgets. 
2. The report pinpoints Parliament's main requirements in terms of competitiveness, regional policy, R&D, development, agriculture and food safety, and foreign policy. 
3. It is important to avoid sacrificing the appropriations of sub-heading 3b in order to forge genuine European citizenship. 
4. The Council and its Presidency should avoid making cuts in the Preliminary Draft Budget at first reading. 
5. The total amount of commitment appropriations is increasing to EUR 134.4 billion, while the amount of payment appropriations is decreasing by 3.3%. 
6. Additional funding is requested for the SOLVIT network to ensure a high degree of consumer satisfaction. 
7. The proposals submitted by the IMCO Committee for the 2009 budget follow the direction of supporting the single market. 
8. The report has largely emphasized the budgetary insufficiencies regarding the fight against global warming, but not enough on the problem of the food crisis. 
9. The European Union needs to listen more to citizens and keep them better informed about what concerns them on a daily basis. 
10. There is a need for considerable resources in the heading of citizenship and information.
