- The Haug report highlights the lack of correlation between requirements for fighting climate change and the EU budget, which is only 1% of Europe's GDP.
- The Commission is allocating only 0.1% of GDP for climate change, while international reports suggest mobilizing between 0.6 and 1.6% of GDP.
- There is a massive gap between the needs and resources provided for developing countries to combat climate change.
- The report also draws attention to limited margins in many sections, particularly in foreign and security policy.
- The Haug report calls for a reworking of the Preliminary Draft Budget to address deficits and incorporate the conclusions of the European Council on 19-20 June.
