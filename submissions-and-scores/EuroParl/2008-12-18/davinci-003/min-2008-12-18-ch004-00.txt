- The speaker, Markus Pieper, is advocating for a plenary debate on the Greenhouse Gas Emission Allowance Trading System (ETS)
- He believes that the current form of the ETS proposal is not representative of Parliament
- He is concerned about the inconsistencies and ballooning costs that may arise from the proposal
- He is questioning the time pressure that is being put on the Parliament to endorse the amendments to the Commission's proposal
- He is emphasizing the importance of the ETS proposal and the need for democratic debate in view of the consequences of the actions taken
- He is expressing his opposition to the idea of leaving climate policy almost entirely to the Council through a simplified procedure
