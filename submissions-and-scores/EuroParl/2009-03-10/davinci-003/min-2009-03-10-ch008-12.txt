- Summarize the meeting:
1. The report was referred back to the Committee on Development in order to agree on a legal base with the support of a majority of the House.
2. There were differences of interpretation between the Committee on Development and the Committee on Foreign Affairs, the Committee on Legal Affairs and the Internal Market, the legal services of the Parliament, the European Court of Justice, the Council and the Commission.
3. The majority insisted on using the legal base as set out in the amendment.
4. The report was referred back to the Committee on Development to find a proper legal basis for actions that are not pure ODA actions but are still important for development cooperation with developing countries.
5. The money foreseen for the DCI for cooperation in accordance with ODA criteria was frozen.
