- The Council has taken the issue of human rights very seriously, as evidenced in the contractual relations of the European Union with countries in various parts of the world.
- The Cotonou Agreement has resulted in more than 15 cases since 1995 when the Community has partially or fully suspended the application of agreements in relation to some of the countries for non-execution of obligations relating to the human rights clause.
- The Commission has carefully taken note of Parliament's concerns about the democracy and human rights situation in Turkmenistan.
- The Commission is aware of Parliament's concerns regarding the possibility of suspending the agreements.
- The Commission undertakes to monitor closely the human rights situation in Turkmenistan, together with Member States, and to report on a regular basis to the appropriate bodies of Parliament.
- The Council and Commission have agreed to seriously consider following Parliament's lead when it delivers opinions and possibly even calls for the suspension of the agreement.
