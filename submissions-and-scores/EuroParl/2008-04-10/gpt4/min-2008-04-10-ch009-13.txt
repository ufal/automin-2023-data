- The 2007 progress report on the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia was discussed.
- Hannes Swoboda suggested postponing the vote on the report to find a solution regarding the sensitive issue of the country's name.
- Daniel Cohn-Bendit argued that the issue has been ongoing for years and that Macedonia should simply be called Macedonia.
- Martin Schulz supported Swoboda's proposal, stating that compromises can be achieved within a matter of days.
- Erik Meijer, the rapporteur, agreed with Swoboda's proposal and the vote was postponed.
