SUMMARY
- Rule 45(2) was recently amended to prohibit amendments to motions for resolutions contained in own-initiative reports.
- It is interfering with political relationships between political groups in the House.
- The Mikko report on concentration and pluralism in the media is the first report approved under this procedure.
- There has been an attack on freedom of expression in Italy with the sacking of 25 journalists from the only television channel over which Berlusconi does not hold sway.
- There is no reason to get worked up about Parliament taking a decision.
- If Parliament has taken a decision that the majority may deem to have been unwise, it can be changed.
- A rule is observed until it is amended, as is the principle of Parliament.
