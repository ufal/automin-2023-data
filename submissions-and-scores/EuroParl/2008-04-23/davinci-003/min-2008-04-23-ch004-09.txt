- Meeting was about the 2007 progress report on the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia
- The rapporteur proposed to maintain the line of not introducing the use of the abbreviation 'FYROM' as a name
- Vytautas Landsbergis proposed an oral amendment to insert an additional explanation of 'the country under the name of FYROM'
- The proposal was rejected by the Parliament
- The conclusion was to replace all other terms included in amendments by 'former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia'
- It was expected that an agreement with Greece would be reached in 2008 to solve the different views on the names of this state
- The Parliament expressed dissatisfaction with the UN's decision to call the republic 'non-existent'
