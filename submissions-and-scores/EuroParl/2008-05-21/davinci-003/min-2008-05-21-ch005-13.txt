- The Council discussed an amendment to the European Parliament's Committee on Legal Affairs
- The amendment states that when environmental damage is caused by a continuing activity, the question of whether the perpetrator acted intentionally or negligently should be determined by the time they became aware of the facts constituting the offence
- The Council understands the intentions expressed in the amendment and believes that Member States will take them into consideration
- The Commission also took note of the amendment and understands the concerns expressed
- The amendment is part of a compromise that was agreed upon and will be approved by the Council
- The Council is confident that the amendment will lead to a legislative success
