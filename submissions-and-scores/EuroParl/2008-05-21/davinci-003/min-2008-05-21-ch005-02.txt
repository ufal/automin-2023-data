- Parliament's calendar of part-sessions for 2009 was discussed and voted on
- An amendment was proposed by Mr Trakatellis, Mr Varvitsiotis, Mr Papastamkos, Mr Lambrinidis and other Members regarding one particular week in the year 2009, namely week 16, from 13 to 19 April
- This amendment was not admissible as there was no proposal for the European Parliament to meet in this particular week
- Mr Daul proposed an amendment to the calendar of part-sessions for 2009, to allow the Members concerned to celebrate the Orthodox Easter
- Mr Swoboda suggested voting on the amendment or at least putting it to the vote
- Mr Nassauer pointed out a problem which affects the vote and which may need to be discussed and resolved
- Mr Cohn-Bendit proposed to change the date of the constituent sitting as it is one of the most important secular holidays
- Parliament agreed to accept the oral amendment
