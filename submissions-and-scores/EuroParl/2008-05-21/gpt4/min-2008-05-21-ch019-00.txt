- Farm structure surveys are essential for providing information on agricultural holdings in the European Union.
- The enlarged EU, including twelve new Member States, requires a new and comprehensive survey.
- Comparable and uniform data is needed to create an equitable and properly functioning agricultural policy.
- The goal is to reach an agreement at the first reading and begin the surveys as soon as possible.
- Amendments resulting from the trialogue have been put into block one, and members are asked to support these amendments.
