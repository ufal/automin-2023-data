The following spoke on the vote:
- Charles Tannock, who moved an oral amendment to recital B, which was
accepted. Marios Matsakis then proposed a change to the wording of that
oral amendment, which Charles Tannock opposed (the President noted this
opposition and confirmed that only the text as modified by the oral
amendment in its author's original wording would be put to the vote);

- Kathalijne Maria Buitenweg, on the procedure followed;

-  Charles Tannock, who moved an oral amendment to recital D, which Marios
Matsakis opposed (the President recalled the provisions in force on oral
amendments), and which was accepted;

- Kathalijne Maria Buitenweg on the procedure followed.

                                      
                                      

The following spoke: Vronique De Keyser, who asked about the way in which
the political groups conduct negotiations on joint resolutions, Marios
Matsakis, Proinsias De Rossa, who spoke out against what he viewed as an
excessive use of oral amendments during the current voting time, Luisa
Morgantini, Bernd Posselt, Marcin Libicki and Paulo Casaca, who referred to
the remarks made by Luisa Morgantini.

The President stated that the remarks made on the use of oral amendments
would be passed on to the relevant authorities, but that in any case the
Rules had been followed correctly.
